Coronavirus Emergency Management Best Practices

We've compiled best practices and lessons learned that communities faced fighting COVID-19. This collection is not exhaustive, but we will continue to add content and highlight key areas.
  • English

The COVID-19 incident period ended on May 11, 2023. FEMA will continue to provide funeral assistance until Sept. 30, 2025, to those who have lost loved ones due to this pandemic.

Community Innovations

Many communities responded to COVID-19 in innovative ways that highlighted the best of American ingenuity.

  • An Alabama-based company creates custom-made, digitally-fitted masks that meld to every contour of a person's face.
  • A new platform matches elder or immunocompromised residents in Oakland, California, with low-risk volunteers who can help them during the COVID-19 pandemic.
  • The University of Oregon's Institute for Health in the Built Environment is piloting the incorporation of regular testing of building surfaces and air ducts for the virus.

Helping People

  • Disability Considerations
    Guidance for state, local, tribal, and territorial governments on how to ensure that people with disabilities have adequate access to goods and services during the COVID-19 pandemic.
  • Connecting Consumers with Local Food
    Countries, cities and private citizens expanded local food production through family and community gardens to supplement supply chains to address COVID-19 pandemic related effects on traditional farming and food supply chains.
  • Sustaining Nutritional Needs for At-Risk Individuals
    Millions of Americans rely on a range of at-home and community-based food services. This resource provides federal agencies and state, local, tribal and territorial governments tools to sustain and expand home delivered meals and other nutrition programs during the COVID-19 pandemic, especially for at-risk individuals.
