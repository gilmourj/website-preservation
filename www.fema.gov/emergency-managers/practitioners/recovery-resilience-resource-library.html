Recovery and Resilience Resource Library

FEMA developed the Recovery and Resilience Resource Library in collaboration with our federal interagency partners to navigate the numerous programs available to the United States and its territories to help recover from a disaster.

The resources are intended for state, local, territorial and tribal (SLTT) governments, as well as nonprofits, businesses, healthcare institutions, schools, individuals and households. The tool helps users to find and research federal disaster recovery resources that would be beneficial in pre-disaster recovery planning or in the wake of a disaster.

NOTICE
Resources provided are for informational purposes only and are compiled with publicly available information. Please directly consult the provider of a potential resource for current program information and to verify the applicability and requirements of a particular program. When you go to another website you are subject to its privacy policies. FEMA does not endorse any non-government websites, companies, or applications.

To provide information about programs of assistance available to individuals, businesses, & public entities after a disaster incident. Programs help individuals cope with losses & affected businesses & public entities restore structures & operations.

Federal Emergency Management Agency - Public Assistance

FEMA may provide assistance for emergency protective measures to state, local, territorial and tribal entities. Emergency Protective Measures eliminate or lessen immediate threats to lives, public health or safety.

Emergency Operations Planning: FEMA Dam Incident Planning Guide (Published: 21 November 2019)

Federal Emergency Management Agency - Public Assistance

Supports state, local, tribal, and territorial emergency managers in planning for dam incidents and failures by summarizing the concepts that a community should consider when creating dam incident-specific elements of local emergency operations plans.

Public Assistance 406 Mitigation (97.036)

Federal Emergency Management Agency - Public Assistance

Funds work to protect damaged facilities against future damage, encourage hazard-resistant design.

National Dam Safety Program (97.041)

Federal Emergency Management Agency - Public Assistance

Funds provided to promote establishment & maintenance of effective state dam safety programs to reduce the risk to human life, property, & the environment from dam related hazards.

Community Disaster Loan (CDL) Program (97.030)

Federal Emergency Management Agency - Public Assistance

Loans to local governments suffering a substantial loss of tax and other revenues to maintain existing functions of a municipal operating character. The local government must demonstrate a need for financial assistance.

Fire Management Assistance Grants

Federal Emergency Management Agency - Public Assistance

Available to states, local and tribal governments, for the mitigation, management, and control of fires on publicly or privately owned forests or grasslands, which threaten such destruction as would constitute a major disaster.

The order encourages agencies, to the extent permitted by law, to transfer computers and related peripheral equipment excess to their needs directly to schools and educational nonprofit organizations.

GSA Customer Service Directors

General Services Administration - General Services Administration

GSA offers a worldwide network of Customer Service Directors (CSDs) knowledgeable in all GSA Solutions.

Programs for State & Local Governments (including Disaster Purchasing Program)

General Services Administration - General Services Administration

State and local governments can purchase equipment and services to support natural or man-made disasters, including acts of terrorism, or nuclear, biological, chemical, or radiological attack.

Assistance Listings [formerly Catalog of Federal Domestic Assistance (CFDA) numbers]

General Services Administration - General Services Administration

A government-wide compendium of Federal programs, projects, services, and activities that provide assistance or benefits to the American public. It contains financial and nonfinancial assistance programs.

Donation of Federal Surplus Personal Property (39.003)

General Services Administration - General Services Administration

To donate federal personal property no longer required for federal use to public agencies & qualifying nonprofit entities to reduce operating costs of state & local government while fully reutilizing the property thus maximizing tax dollars.

Disposal of Federal Surplus Real Property (39.002)

General Services Administration - Real Property Activities

To assist state and local governments, and certain eligible tax-exempt status nonprofit organizations with acquiring surplus federal real property for various public uses that benefit communities.

U.S. Government Open Data: Data.gov

General Services Administration - Technology Transformation Services

Searchable data sets. Data, tools, and resources to conduct research, develop web and mobile applications, design data visualizations, and other functions.

Collections Assessment for Preservation Program

Institute of Museum and Library Services

Grant funding to facilitate the sharing and integration of information about impacts to natural and cultural collections to aid in understanding recovery needs and support decisions by impacted stakeholders.

Museum Assessment Program (MAP)

Institute of Museum and Library Services

The Museum Assessment Program (MAP) is designed to help museums assess their strengths and weaknesses, and plan for the future.

Creative Forces: Military Healing Arts Network (45.024)

National Endowment for the Arts

This initiative seeks to improve the health, wellness, and quality of life for military and veteran populations exposed to trauma - as well as their families and caregivers - using creative arts therapies.

Mayor's Institute on City Design (MICD) (45.024)

National Endowment for the Arts

The MICD brings U.S. mayors together with design professionals in workshops to assist mayors with urban design challenges, to promote design excellence, and to enhance the livability of communities across the country.

Challenge America Grant Program (45.024)

National Endowment for the Arts

Challenge America offers support primarily to small organizations for projects in all artistic disciplines that extend the reach of the arts to populations that are underserved.

Citizens' Institute on Rural Design (CIRD)

Provides a forum for rural technical assistance providers to share their professional skills, learn new techniques that would aid them in their work with rural communities, and exchange ideas and experiences with rural communities and other providers.

Literature Fellowships: Translation Projects (45.024)

Funding to individuals supports projects for the translation of specific works of prose, poetry, or drama from other languages into English.
